Habitat

Lemurs are exclusively found on the island of Madagascar and nearby Comoros Islands. Their habitat ranges from rainforests and dry forests to spiny forests and mountainous regions. Due to Madagascar's diverse ecosystems, lemurs have adapted to a variety of environments, showcasing their versatility in adapting to different ecological niches.

Diet

Lemurs are omnivores, and their diet varies based on their species and habitat. They consume a mix of fruits, leaves, flowers, insects, and small vertebrates. The aye-aye, with its specialized finger, is particularly adept at extracting insect larvae from tree bark.
